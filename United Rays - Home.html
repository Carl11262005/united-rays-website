Our Story

Built on Vision, Driven by Family

From our start in 1977 to today, the United Rays have rolled on because of the vision of our forebears and the dedication of our family behind the wheel.

We are a diverse group with one thing in common — a love for the Corvette. But the cars are only the beginning. The real highlight is the people: a family with a wealth of skills, talents, and a shared commitment to the integrity of the organization.

Whether we're competing on the national stage, cruising the Steel City streets, or giving back to the Pittsburgh community, we carry the same pride that has defined the United Rays for nearly half a century.

"Where 'United' is our focus."

The Club Code

The Ten Commandments

Of the Corvette Owner · Honored by the United Rays

I

Thou shalt not fail to wave to a fellow Corvette owner.

II

Thou shalt not pass a fellow Corvette owner in distress without offering aid.

III

Thou shalt not feed thy Corvette budget-grade fuel.

IV

Thou shalt treat thy Vette with respect; great power, great responsibility.

V

Thou shalt keep thy Corvette clean and waxed at all times, yet thou shalt not bring thy Corvette to an automatic car wash.

VI

Thou shalt park thy Corvette in such a manner as to protect her tender fenders.

VII

Thou shalt blow out carbon with a high-speed run as required.

VIII

Thou shalt not cruise on an unpaved road.

IX

Neither friend, nor foe, nor even thy spouse may drive thy Corvette without first showing proper reverence.

X

Each Vette is an individual work of art; judge not lest ye might be judged.
